Question 1: List 5 examples of software errors related to loss of money, time, or business reputation, injury or death. Explain.
1.	Ariane 5 – Loss of money ($7 billion):
The Ariane 5 rocket exploded because of a software error when converting a 64-bit floating-point number into a 16-bit integer, causing an overflow. As a result, the entire spacecraft worth about $7 billion was lost.
2.	Mariner 1 Space Probe ($250 million) – Loss of money:
The Venus space probe was destroyed on July 22, 1962 due to a coding error (a missing hyphen in the navigation formula), causing a loss of approximately $250 million.
3.	American Airlines ($50 million) – Business reputation & money:
A software system failure at American Airlines caused losses of around $50 million, possibly related to booking or operational systems. It also negatively affected the company’s reputation.
4.	Therac-25 – Injury or death:
The Therac-25 radiation therapy machine had software control errors that caused patients to receive radiation doses many times higher than the safe limit, resulting in severe injuries and deaths.
5.	Banking system error causing suicide – Death & reputation:
A banking system bug sent incorrect overdraft notifications to customers. One customer committed suicide because of the false information, causing both loss of life and serious damage to the bank’s reputation.

Question 3: Difference between Verification and Validation. Give examples.
Verification	Validation
Checking whether the product is built correctly according to specifications and requirements	Checking whether the product meets the real needs of users
Question: “Are we building the product right?”	Question: “Are we building the right product?”
Focuses on process, documents, and design	Focuses on the final product and real users
Examples
•	Verification:
Does the shirt have two sleeves? Is it size L? Is it blue? Are any buttons missing?
→ Checking based on specifications.
•	Validation:
Does the shirt fit comfortably? Is it comfortable to wear? Does the color suit the user?
→ Checking whether it is suitable for real users.
